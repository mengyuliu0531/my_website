What do I do in my spare time?

Making everyday journals

I love writing down every details of things I have done for each day. I regard this as making every minutes, every hours of my life meaningful, as when I come back, I am able to tell exactly what I did on a certain afternoon, what did I eat for that dinner, was I happy or disapointed and why, etc. I really enjoy the time I recall things I have experienced.

I have mainly use three type of journals: daily, weekly and timeline. Usually I start a new book when a year starts, and I would choose a certain type for each year based on what will my life look like for the following year.

Here are some examples:

Timeline

Basically, timeline journal is like a schedule book where you write things you need to do for each hour of the day. However, my timeline journal is more than just a to do list.

timeline journal 2021.10

First, timeline shows what I have done for today, such as taking courses, doing homework, visiting museum and so forth. Below the timeline, I write down the urgent things like due for today, with a checkbox. At the bottom, I record three meals of the day.

I use timeline journal when the life is rich, such as my life at LBS. I have lots of courses, assignments and events here in London, therefore, I believe timeline journal is the best choice.

Daily

Daily looks like timeline, but it is more colourful and illustrative with more vivid images.

daily journal 2019.5

I was not that busy in 2019, when I was a sophomore. I would use different colours to distinguish different courses: mandatory, electives, major and minor. I bought some pretty loveable stickers that time, so I put them into my daily journal, to show clearly what I have eaten for meals. Hence the journal looks fantastic.

Weekly

Weekly literally means you do not care too much about what you do every day, instead, you record your life more on a weekly basis.

weekly journal 2020.10

Weekly journals provides more space for you to design your journal freely. I chose to write down the meaningful things of the day, with stickers representing foods and my everyday feelings: happy, sad or upset. This kind of journal definitely requires more time to design and compose, so you probably won’t choose weekly when you are extremely overwhelmed your work. I made this journal when I took exchange program in Japan, where the life pace was pretty low, thus I owned lots of time to do this.

Dancing

I like dancing, especially with k-pop music. It is a fantastic way to exercise, but completely with more fun, since you can really perform something at the end. I usually learn to dance in a dance studio in Beijing, about twice a week. Here in the UK, my roommates and I go to the studio in school’s gym once a week, to learn dancing with tutoring videos.
